## MBS Telehealth Items Overview

Telehealth items were significantly expanded during and following the COVID-19 pandemic. Telehealth consultations are now a permanent feature of the MBS. Key telehealth item categories relevant to Nightingale GP consultations are listed below.

## GP Standard Telehealth Consultations

**Level A (brief — telephone only, items 91790/91800):**
- Duration: typically 5 minutes or less
- Telephone only (not video)
- Item 91790: GP telephone consultation — surgery attendance Level A equivalent
- Item 91800: after hours equivalent

**Level B (standard — telephone or video, items 91891/91892):**
- Duration: 5–20 minutes (Level B equivalent)
- Telephone or video; most common telehealth consultation item
- Item 91891: telehealth consultation, Level B standard
- Item 91892: after hours equivalent
- Bulk-billing incentive available for concession card holders and children under 16

**Level C (long — telephone or video, items 91893/91894):**
- Duration: 20–40 minutes
- Telephone or video
- Item 91893: telehealth consultation, Level C standard
- Item 91894: after hours equivalent

**Level D (prolonged — telephone or video, items 91895/91896):**
- Duration: 40+ minutes
- Item 91895: telehealth consultation, Level D standard
- Item 91896: after hours equivalent

**Level E (very prolonged — telephone or video, item 91897):**
- Duration: 60+ minutes
- Complex case management telehealth

## Mental Health Telehealth Items

**GP Mental Health Treatment Plan (MHTP) telehealth:**
- Item 2717: telehealth GP Mental Health Treatment Plan (equivalent of 2715 in-person)
- Item 2712: MHTP review via telehealth

**Allied Health / Psychology telehealth under Better Access:**
- Item 80010–80030 series: clinical psychologist telehealth sessions (10 per calendar year; additional 10 after GP review)
- Item 80100–80115 series: registered psychologist telehealth sessions
- Item 80125–80140 series: mental health social worker / occupational therapist telehealth sessions

## Chronic Disease Management (CDM) Telehealth

**GP Management Plan / Team Care Arrangement (TCA):**
- Item 229: telehealth GP Management Plan (equivalent of 721 in-person)
- Item 230: telehealth TCA (equivalent of 723)
- Item 232: telehealth contribution to multidisciplinary care plan

**Allied health CDM sessions (telehealth):**
- Items 10950–10970: physiotherapy, occupational therapy, speech pathology, podiatry via telehealth under TCA; 5 sessions per calendar year total (combined team)

## Specialist Telehealth Items (Reference)

**Specialist consultation telehealth:**
- Item 104: specialist consultation level B via telehealth (not GP — specialist to GP communication)
- Note: GP-to-specialist referral for telehealth consultation follows standard referral pathway; specialist bills their own telehealth items to the patient/Medicare

## Telehealth Eligibility and Requirements

**Patient eligibility:**
- All Medicare cardholders eligible for telehealth; no geographical restriction (expanded from 2020)
- Existing clinical relationship: for telephone-only consultations, many items require an existing relationship (patient has attended GP within 12 months); exceptions for new patients in certain circumstances

**Prescriber requirements:**
- Must be a current MBS provider (provider number registered with Services Australia)
- Must use appropriate item numbers matching service duration and type
- Video preferred: for consultations requiring visual assessment; telephone acceptable for many reviews

**Clinical appropriateness:**
- Not all conditions can be appropriately managed via telehealth; GP must assess clinical suitability
- Document: reason for telehealth mode; consent obtained; clinical decision made after telehealth review
- Physical examination required: in-person attendance for specific presentations (see individual condition files for "Requires in-person assessment" guidance)

## Bulk-Billing Incentives

**Incentive items for telehealth:**
- Additional bulk-billing incentive items apply for:
  - Children under 16 years
  - Concession card holders
  - Patients in rural and remote areas
- Items: specific bulk-billing incentive item numbers assigned based on consultation item; prescribing software auto-populates

## After-Hours Telehealth

- Standard after-hours loading applies to telehealth items as per in-person equivalents
- After-hours definitions: evenings (6 PM–11 PM weekdays), early morning (8 AM–midnight weekends), late night / deep night

## COVID-19 Telehealth Permanent Changes

Following COVID-19 temporary telehealth expansions:
- All patients (not just rural) now eligible for telehealth
- Existing patient relationship requirement maintained for telephone items (some exceptions apply)
- Video capability not mandatory for most items but preferred
