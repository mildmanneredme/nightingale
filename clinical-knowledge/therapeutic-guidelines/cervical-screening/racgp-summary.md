## Cervical Screening Assessment (RACGP)

Australia's National Cervical Screening Program (NCSP) underwent a major overhaul in December 2017, replacing 2-yearly Pap smear testing with 5-yearly HPV (human papillomavirus) testing using the Cervical Screening Test (CST). This shift was based on evidence that HPV testing is substantially more sensitive for detecting high-grade cervical abnormalities than cytology alone, and that persistent HPV infection drives virtually all cervical cancers. The new program has been accompanied by high-efficacy HPV vaccination (Gardasil 9 — NIP-funded from age 12), positioning Australia as the first country in the world on track to eliminate cervical cancer as a public health problem (incidence <4/100,000). The GP plays a central role in cervical screening, result management, colposcopy referral, vaccination promotion, and supporting patients from culturally and linguistically diverse backgrounds and those who have experienced trauma. Telehealth is suitable for result discussion and counselling.

## Clinical Presentation

**Who requires cervical screening:**
- Women and people with a cervix aged 25–74 years who have ever been sexually active
- 5-yearly Cervical Screening Test (HPV primary testing + reflex LBC if HPV positive)
- Not recommended before age 25 (higher rate of transient HPV positivity; spontaneous clearance; overtreatment risk)
- After 74 years: cease screening after two normal tests within the program
- Women who have had hysterectomy: if cervix was removed AND no previous high-grade lesion — may cease screening; if subtotal hysterectomy (cervix retained) — continue
- Pregnant women: defer CST to 6 weeks post-partum unless overdue
- Women who have never had sex with a male partner: much lower risk; discuss individual risk; may consider screening

**Vaccination and screening:**
- HPV-vaccinated women: screening still required (vaccine does not cover all HPV types causing cancer; earlier cohorts may have had limited or no vaccination)
- Gardasil 9: covers HPV 16, 18, 31, 33, 45, 52, 58 (high-risk) and 6, 11 (genital warts)

**Abnormal uterine bleeding — separate issue from screening:**
- Postcoital bleeding, intermenstrual bleeding, postmenopausal bleeding: do not defer as "screening due" — these require clinical assessment and colposcopy regardless of recent normal screen

## Assessment Approach

**Cervical Screening Test (CST) — technique:**
- Liquid-based cytology (LBC) collection with a cervical brush (Cervex-Brush or similar)
- Endocervical sweep and ectocervical sample; specimens stored in liquid preservation medium
- Sample sent to laboratory for HPV primary testing ± reflex LBC if HPV positive
- Ensure test ordered as "Cervical Screening Test" (not Pap smear — different test)

**Self-collection option (2022 NCSP change):**
- People who have never screened or are overdue ≥2 years can self-collect a vaginal swab for HPV testing
- Only HPV testing (not LBC) — if HPV positive, requires clinician-collected sample for reflex LBC
- Increases access for those reluctant or unable to have clinician-collected sample (cultural barriers, trauma history, disability)
- Can be done in clinic or at home (postal self-collection kits available in some jurisdictions)

**Understanding CST results:**
- HPV not detected: repeat in 5 years — routine
- HPV detected (not 16/18) + negative LBC: repeat in 12 months (most will clear)
- HPV detected (not 16/18) + positive LBC (any grade): refer to colposcopy
- HPV 16/18 detected (regardless of LBC): refer to colposcopy — higher cancer risk
- HPV 16 or 18 + HSIL on LBC: urgent colposcopy referral
- Low-grade squamous intraepithelial lesion (LSIL) + HPV 16/18: colposcopy
- High-grade squamous intraepithelial lesion (HSIL) on LBC: urgent colposcopy regardless of HPV type

**Post-colposcopy and post-treatment follow-up:**
- After treatment for HSIL (CIN2/3): follow-up HPV test at 12 months then annually × 2 years; return to 5-yearly if negative
- After hysterectomy for cervical cancer or CIN3: annual vault smear for 10 years — gynaecology-managed

**Recall and follow-up — National Cancer Screening Register (NCSR):**
- NCSR sends automated reminders to patients; GPs can access NCSR for patient recall status
- Overdue patients: proactive recall by GP practice; opportunistic reminder at any consultation

## Key Differentials

- Vulvovaginal candidiasis or BV: vaginal discharge; may be incidental finding; does not affect screening result interpretation
- Cervicitis (chlamydia, gonorrhoea): STI testing; treat before or concurrent with screening; see `sexually-transmitted-infections` entry
- Cervical polyp: visible on speculum examination; benign; arrange gynaecology review for removal if symptomatic
- Cervical cancer: abnormal bleeding, contact bleeding, visible cervical lesion — urgent colposcopy and gynaecology referral regardless of prior screening history; do not defer for scheduled screen
- Endometrial cancer: postmenopausal bleeding; transvaginal ultrasound + endometrial biopsy — not a cervical screen issue

## Management Principles

**Colposcopy referral — when and why:**
- NCSP pathways define colposcopy referral triggers (as above)
- Colposcopy involves magnified examination of cervix + targeted biopsy; performed by gynaecologist or trained colposcopist
- Do not treat LSIL/HSIL without colposcopy and histological confirmation first (except emergency)
- Refer to gynaecology for all recommended colposcopies; include full results summary and screening history

**Cervical cancer treatment (specialist):**
- Early stage (IA1–IB1): cone biopsy (fertility-sparing) or hysterectomy; radiotherapy
- Advanced: concurrent chemoradiation ± brachytherapy; oncology

**HPV vaccination promotion — GP role:**
- Gardasil 9: NIP-funded for all students in Year 7 (males and females, ~12 years); funded catch-up to age 25 for eligible unvaccinated individuals
- Highly effective: >90% reduction in high-grade cervical lesions in vaccinated cohorts
- Recommend vaccination at every appropriate opportunity; safe and effective; 2-dose schedule for 9–14 years; 3-dose for ≥15 years or immunocompromised
- Immunocompromised individuals (HIV, solid organ transplant): higher risk of HPV-related disease; may still benefit from vaccination at any age — consult ATAGI recommendations
- Discuss with patients who had prior genital warts or HPV infection — may still benefit (covers multiple types)

**Culturally safe practice:**
- Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander women: cervical cancer incidence 2× higher; mortality 3× higher; cultural barriers to screening; self-collection option especially valuable; engage Aboriginal community health workers
- CALD communities: interpreter services; translated resources (Cancer Council Australia)
- Survivors of sexual trauma: trauma-informed approach; self-collection option; offer breaks during procedure

## Prescribing Considerations (AU PBS)

- **Gardasil 9 (HPV vaccine)** (NIP-funded: Year 7 school program males and females; funded catch-up for eligible persons; private prescription if outside NIP eligibility; note: does not substitute for screening)
- **Note:** Cervical screening itself is a Medicare-rebatable pathology service — MBS Item 73070 (CST); no prescription required
- **LLETZ / cone biopsy**: surgical procedures performed by gynaecologist; not GP-prescribable
- Ensure patients understand screening is separate from vaccination — both required

## Escalation Triggers

**Immediate urgent colposcopy referral:**
- HPV 16/18 detected on any CST result
- HSIL on reflex LBC
- Visible cervical lesion suspicious for malignancy — regardless of screening status
- Abnormal postcoital or postmenopausal bleeding with cervical abnormality

**Semi-urgent colposcopy referral:**
- HPV detected (non-16/18) + positive reflex LBC (any grade)
- LSIL on LBC with HPV 16/18

**Routine 12-month recall:**
- HPV detected (non-16/18) + negative LBC — repeat CST in 12 months; refer if still positive at 12 months

**Non-urgent referral / coordination:**
- Gynaecology: colposcopy referral for all above-threshold results
- Women's health nurse / Indigenous health workers: cultural support and assisted screening programs
- Survivor of trauma — psychologist support if pelvic examination causes distress

## Telehealth Suitability

Cervical screening result discussion is well suited to telehealth; the physical procedure requires in-person attendance.

**Suitable for telehealth:**
- CST result discussion and counselling
- Explanation of HPV testing and next steps
- Colposcopy referral coordination
- HPV vaccination counselling
- Self-collection eligibility assessment and postal kit arrangement (some jurisdictions)
- Recall and overdue patient engagement

**Requires in-person assessment:**
- Cervical Screening Test collection (speculum examination)
- Initial clinical assessment for abnormal bleeding or visible cervical lesion
- Self-collection in clinic (alternative to clinician-collected)
