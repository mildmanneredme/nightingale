## Opioid Stewardship Assessment (RACGP)

Opioid stewardship encompasses the responsible prescribing, monitoring, and management of opioid analgesics to optimise patient outcomes and minimise harms. Australia has experienced a significant increase in opioid-related harms: opioid-related overdose deaths increased 350% over 20 years; approximately 70% of fatal overdoses involve pharmaceutical opioids (not heroin); codeine was moved to Prescription Only (Schedule 4) in 2018. RACGP Guidelines for Prescribing Opioids for Chronic Non-Cancer Pain (2021) provide evidence-based guidance. Key principles: opioids provide modest and often diminishing benefit for chronic non-cancer pain (CNCP); functional improvement rather than pain score is the primary goal; risk of dependence, tolerance, and opioid-induced hyperalgesia increases with dose and duration; SafeScript (real-time prescription monitoring) is mandatory in most states; tapering of ineffective or high-dose opioids is a core GP skill. This entry covers safe opioid initiation, monitoring, tapering, and recognition of problematic use. See also `opioid-dependence` for OAT management. Telehealth is suitable for opioid review consultations.

## Clinical Presentation

**Appropriate opioid use scenarios:**
- Acute severe pain (post-operative, trauma, cancer pain): short-term; clearly defined duration; regular review
- Cancer pain: palliative context; WHO analgesic ladder; less controversy
- Chronic non-cancer pain (CNCP): most problematic; requires RACGP 2021 guidelines approach; reassess benefit/harm ratio

**Indicators of problematic opioid use:**
- Requesting early refills; "lost" scripts; multiple prescribers (SafeScript)
- Escalating doses without functional improvement; dose exceeds guideline thresholds
- Diversion: selling or giving prescriptions to others; multiple pharmacies
- Signs of intoxication: sedation, miosis, slurred speech, cognitive impairment
- Withdrawal symptoms between doses: sweating, piloerection, agitation, craving, GI symptoms
- Aberrant behaviours: doctor-shopping; prescription forgery; stealing medications

**SafeScript (real-time prescription monitoring — RTPM):**
- Mandatory checks in Victoria (2020+), expanding to all states; check before prescribing Schedule 8 opioids
- Identifies: multiple prescribers; high doses; concerning prescription patterns
- Required action if concerning pattern: patient review; clinical documentation; mandatory reporting in some circumstances

**High-risk populations:**
- History of substance use disorder (including alcohol): higher risk of OUD
- Mental health comorbidity (depression, PTSD, anxiety, personality disorder): complex pain-mood interaction
- Sleep apnoea: increased respiratory depression risk, especially with sedatives
- Respiratory disease (COPD, asthma): increased overdose risk
- Older adults (≥65): falls, cognitive impairment, reduced opioid clearance; lower doses required
- Concurrent benzodiazepines: fatal combination; evidence for markedly increased overdose mortality

## Assessment Approach

**Opioid initiation assessment:**
- Confirm diagnosis and failure of non-opioid therapies: opioids are not first-line for most CNCP; trial of paracetamol, NSAIDs, topical agents, physiotherapy, CBT first
- ORT (Opioid Risk Tool): predict likelihood of aberrant opioid-related behaviour; 5-item questionnaire; low (<4), moderate (4–7), high risk (≥8)
- SafeScript check: mandatory before initiation in applicable states
- Baseline functional assessment: pain score AND function (work, social, sleep, daily activities) — functional improvement is treatment goal
- Psychological assessment: depression, anxiety, catastrophising (PCS), PTSD
- Consent: opioid patient agreement (documented); risks, benefits, goals, monitoring, conditions for cessation
- Urine drug screen (UDS): baseline before initiation for high-risk patients; confirms compliance

**Ongoing monitoring:**
- Regular review: monthly initially; reassess every 3–6 months
- Assess: pain, function, quality of life, side effects, aberrant behaviours
- SafeScript: check at each prescription
- Urine drug screen: regularly in high-risk patients; random
- Dose calculation: convert all opioids to morphine milligram equivalents (MME) per day
- High-dose threshold: >50 MME/day requires enhanced monitoring; >90 MME/day requires specialist review or pain clinic

**Morphine milligram equivalents (MME) conversion:**
- Oxycodone: × 1.5 (10 mg oxycodone = 15 MME)
- Hydromorphone: × 5 (2 mg hydromorphone = 10 MME)
- Fentanyl patch 25 µg/hr = 60 MME/day
- Codeine: × 0.15 (30 mg codeine = 4.5 MME)
- Buprenorphine sublingual (analgesic): calculate via specialist tool

## Key Clinical Considerations

**Acute vs chronic pain prescribing:**
- Acute pain: appropriate short-term; immediate-release opioids; limit supply; 3–7 days post-procedural; review; do NOT provide large supply "just in case"
- Cancer pain: WHO ladder (Step 1: non-opioid → Step 2: weak opioid → Step 3: strong opioid ± adjuvants); no ceiling for cancer pain; palliative care team
- CNCP: most controversial; opioids often prescribed but evidence for long-term benefit modest; functional improvement not just pain reduction is the goal

**Evidence for opioids in CNCP (RACGP 2021 key findings):**
- Opioids provide modest short-term pain relief for CNCP; evidence for long-term benefit is poor
- Functional improvement is not consistently demonstrated in long-term studies
- Harms increase with dose: dependence, overdose, falls, cognitive impairment, endocrine effects, constipation
- Opioid-induced hyperalgesia (OIH): paradoxical increase in pain sensitivity with prolonged opioid use
- Long-acting (sustained-release) opioids: NOT superior to short-acting for CNCP; higher overdose risk; prefer IR formulations for CNCP where opioids are appropriate

## Management Principles

**Initiating opioids for CNCP (when other treatments have failed):**
- Start with lowest effective dose; short-acting (immediate-release) formulation; regular reassessment
- Document: treatment goals (functional targets); consent for monitoring; conditions for dose escalation and cessation
- Trial period: reassess at 4–8 weeks; continue only if functional improvement documented
- Avoid: benzodiazepines concurrent (naloxone required if combined); avoid long-acting formulations as first-line

**Prescribing safe doses:**
- Threshold 1 (enhanced monitoring): >50 MME/day
- Threshold 2 (seek specialist input): >90 MME/day
- Consider: dose reduction, opioid rotation, multimodal pain management, pain clinic referral at threshold 2
- Codeine (post-2018 Rx-only): ceiling dose 240 mg/day codeine (equivalent to 36 MME); limited evidence for CNCP; step-down to non-opioid preferred

**Opioid tapering (dose reduction):**
- Indications: pain not improved, functional decline, side effects, aberrant behaviours, requested by patient
- Taper method: 10–20% reduction every 1–4 weeks (slower in patients on higher doses or longer duration)
- Patient-centred: involve patient in planning; agree on timeline; support with adjuvant therapies
- Withdrawal management: adjunctive clonidine 0.1–0.3 mg BD–TDS (reduces autonomic withdrawal symptoms); reassurance; short-term low-dose benzodiazepine ONLY under supervision for severe withdrawal
- Do NOT abruptly cease opioids in opioid-dependent patient: precipitates acute withdrawal (not life-threatening in most cases, but severely distressing)
- Pain management during taper: ramp up non-opioid analgesia; physiotherapy; CBT for pain; interdisciplinary pain clinic

**Patient counselling (opioid safety):**
- Never drive or operate machinery while sedated (opioids impair driving; legal requirement in Australia)
- Do NOT combine with alcohol or benzodiazepines: greatly increases overdose risk
- Storage: locked cabinet; away from children and visitors; Drug Misuse and Trafficking Act requirements
- Disposal: return unused opioids to pharmacy; RUM project (Return Unwanted Medicines)
- Overdose risk: signs (unrousable, blue lips, slow breathing); family member naloxone training

**Naloxone (Nyxoid, Narcan) co-prescribing:**
- RACGP 2021 recommends: co-prescribe naloxone to all patients on ≥50 MME/day or concurrent sedative medications
- Nyxoid nasal spray 1.8 mg: PBS-listed since 2020; family members trained in use; rapid reversal of opioid overdose; call 000 first
- Can be dispensed without prescription from pharmacist under a pharmacist standing order in some states

## Prescribing Considerations (AU PBS)

- **Oxycodone IR 5–10 mg (Endone)** (PBS restricted Schedule 8; post-surgical acute pain; short-acting; limit supply; SafeScript check; written Authority required; review at 4–8 weeks)
- **Oxycodone-naloxone CR (Targin)** (PBS restricted: CNCP; naloxone component reduces opioid-induced constipation; Schedule 8; requires Authority)
- **Fentanyl transdermal patch (Durogesic)** (PBS restricted: cancer pain and severe CNCP; Schedule 8; requires high-risk management plan; safety risk in accidental paediatric exposure)
- **Naloxone nasal spray (Nyxoid) 1.8 mg** (PBS restricted: opioid overdose reversal; family/carer use; train caregiver; keep near patient at all times if on high-dose opioids)
- **Note:** SafeScript (real-time prescription monitoring) is mandatory before prescribing Schedule 8 opioids in Victoria and progressively in all Australian states. Non-compliance with RTPM requirements may breach prescribing conditions and is a risk management issue.

## Escalation Triggers

**Emergency:**
- Opioid overdose (unconscious, unresponsive, slow breathing, blue lips): 000; administer Nyxoid nasal spray if available while waiting for ambulance; rescue breathing
- Acute opioid withdrawal with severe complications: IV fluids; medical supervision

**Urgent referral:**
- Suspected opioid use disorder (OUD): opioid agonist therapy (buprenorphine-naloxone/methadone); urgent referral to `opioid-dependence`; SafeScript alert
- Opioid dose >90 MME/day with inadequate clinical response: pain clinic referral; specialist input
- Benzodiazepine + opioid combination at high dose: deprescribe one; naloxone co-prescribe immediately

**Non-urgent:**
- Interdisciplinary pain clinic: chronic non-cancer pain with inadequate response to primary care management; CNCP + mental health comorbidity
- Addiction medicine / substance use services: complex OUD; illicit drug use comorbidity

## Telehealth Suitability

**Suitable for telehealth:**
- SafeScript check and opioid prescription renewal (after recent in-person review)
- Opioid taper progress monitoring; withdrawal support
- Pain management review; analgesic adjustments
- Naloxone counselling and prescription
- Psychosocial support and referral to pain clinic

**Requires in-person assessment:**
- Initial opioid prescription: full history, ORT assessment, functional status, documentation
- Urine drug screen collection
- Assessment of sedation, gait, cognitive impairment (falls risk with opioids)
