## Developmental Concerns Assessment (RACGP)

Developmental surveillance is a core component of Australian child health practice, embedded in the Maternal and Child Health (MCH) nursing program (Victoria) and similar state programs. GPs play a key role in identifying developmental delay, autism spectrum disorder (ASD), ADHD, and other neurodevelopmental conditions during routine health checks, vaccination appointments, and parent-initiated consultations. The developmental domains assessed are: gross motor, fine motor, speech and language, cognition, personal-social, and adaptive behaviour. Global developmental delay (GDD) involves delay in ≥2 domains; specific delays affect individual domains. ASD (see below) is the most common neurodevelopmental condition requiring specific assessment pathways. Early diagnosis and intervention is critical: evidence shows early intervention produces the best outcomes. Referral pathways: GP → NDIS early childhood early intervention (ECEI) → Speech pathology, OT, paediatric physiotherapy, developmental paediatrician, child psychiatry. Telehealth has limited utility for developmental assessment; in-person observation is usually required.

## Clinical Presentation

**Developmental red flags (require urgent referral):**
- ANY loss of previously achieved developmental milestone at any age: urgent assessment; consider regression, ASD, metabolic or neurological cause
- No smile by 2 months
- No babbling by 9 months
- No single words by 16 months
- No 2-word phrases by 24 months
- No pointing, waving, or pretend play by 18 months (ASD concern)
- No words at 18 months or no sentences at 24 months

**Global developmental delay (GDD):**
- Delay in 2 or more developmental domains: gross motor, fine motor, speech/language, cognition, personal-social
- GDD defined formally as performance >2 SDs below mean on standardised tests in ≥2 domains (assessment by developmental paediatrician)
- Causes: chromosomal disorders (Down syndrome, fragile X), perinatal hypoxia, congenital infections, metabolic diseases, ASD, environmental deprivation
- Investigation: hearing test (MANDATORY); vision screen; chromosomal microarray; metabolic screen; MRI brain if regression or focal signs

**Speech and language delay:**
- Most common developmental concern presented to GPs
- Expressive delay only: often resolves with intervention; lower concern for ASD
- Mixed expressive + receptive delay: higher concern; broader assessment needed
- ALWAYS: check hearing (otitis media with effusion is common cause); Hearing Australia audiological testing; chronic OME → grommets (see `hearing-loss`)
- Referral: speech pathology for all speech delay regardless of severity

**Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD):**
- Prevalence: approximately 1 in 70 Australian children (2023); higher in males (4:1 ratio)
- Core features (DSM-5): deficits in social communication and social interaction; restricted, repetitive behaviours; symptoms present in early developmental period
- Early ASD signs (12–24 months): reduced eye contact, reduced social smile, not pointing or waving, not responding to name, repetitive play with objects, inflexible routines, unusual sensory responses
- Modified Checklist for Autism in Toddlers Revised (M-CHAT-R): validated screening tool; use at 18–24-month well child visit; free download
- Regression: approximately 25–30% of ASD children show regression (loss of previously acquired language at 18–24 months)
- Formal diagnosis: developmental paediatrician, child psychiatrist, or multidisciplinary team; ADI-R and ADOS-2 gold standard assessment tools

**ADHD (see also `adhd-adult` for adults):**
- Children most commonly diagnosed at school entry (5–7 years) when demands on attention and self-regulation become evident
- Subtypes: predominantly inattentive, predominantly hyperactive-impulsive, combined
- School performance concerns; teacher reports; SNAP-IV or Conners questionnaire; paediatric assessment required for diagnosis and stimulant prescribing

**Intellectual disability:**
- IQ <70 + significant adaptive behaviour impairment; onset during developmental period
- Causes overlap with GDD; chromosomal microarray; karyotype; metabolic screen; NDIS eligibility

## Assessment Approach

**GP developmental surveillance (at well-child check points):**
- Key assessment ages: 6 weeks, 4 months, 8 months, 12 months, 18 months, 2 years, 3 years, 4 years, school entry (5–6 years)
- Australian Developmental Paediatrics Society surveillance tools: Paediatric Developmental Screening (PEDS) or Ages and Stages Questionnaire (ASQ-3) — can be parent-completed before appointment
- Always ask parents: "Do you have any concerns about your child's development, learning, or behaviour?" — parental concern is the most sensitive predictor of developmental disorder

**Developmental history (structured):**
- Motor milestones: head control (4 months), sitting (6–7 months), walking (12 months); delay >2 months = refer
- Speech milestones: social smile (6 weeks), cooing (2–3 months), babbling (6 months), first words (12 months), 2-word phrases (24 months), 3+ word sentences (36 months)
- Social: eye contact, social smile, interest in other children, imitation, pretend play, empathy
- Adaptive: dressing, toilet training, feeding

**Direct observation:**
- Observe child during consultation: engagement, eye contact, response to name, play skills, vocalisations
- M-CHAT-R: complete at 18–24 months visits; positive screen = referral

**Investigations:**
- Hearing test: MANDATORY for ANY speech delay or concern; Hearing Australia audiological assessment; newborn hearing screening (all Australian states)
- Vision screen: cover test; Snellen chart (age appropriate); ophthalmology referral if concern
- Chromosomal microarray: first-line for unexplained GDD or suspected genetic syndrome (replaces standard karyotype for most indications)
- Fragile X DNA test: boys with GDD, large head, prominent ears, family history
- Metabolic screen: if specific metabolic disease features or regression
- MRI brain: neurological symptoms; seizures; regression; specific motor signs
- Thyroid function: hypothyroidism can cause GDD

## Key Differentials

- Global developmental delay: multiple domain involvement; chromosome microarray; metabolic screen; MRI
- Autism spectrum disorder: social communication deficit + restricted behaviour; M-CHAT-R; developmental paediatrician
- Hearing impairment: speech delay WITHOUT social delay (differentiates from ASD); hearing test urgently
- ADHD: attention and executive function; teacher report; no social communication deficit (unlike ASD); stimulant medication after paediatric diagnosis
- Cerebral palsy: motor disorder from early brain lesion; abnormal tone and reflexes; physiotherapy + neurology
- Intellectual disability: global cognitive impairment; formal psychological assessment; NDIS support
- Environmental deprivation or neglect: language delay without neurological signs; intervention improves outcomes; child protection if neglect suspected

## Management Principles

**Early referral and intervention (cornerstone):**
- Early intervention services: most benefit before age 5; neuroplasticity highest in first years; delay in referral = delay in intervention
- NDIS Early Childhood Early Intervention (ECEI): children 0–6 with developmental concerns or disability; access without formal diagnosis; GP referral letter (NDIS planning request)
- NDIS: children 0–6 with developmental delay or disability; early childhood partner (Local Area Coordinator — LAC); supports: speech pathology, OT, developmental paediatrician reviews, social support for families
- Medicare: Chronic Disease Management (CDM) items can fund allied health visits for children with developmental delay; GP Management Plan + Team Care Arrangement

**Speech pathology:**
- Refer all children with speech delay regardless of cause or severity
- Speech pathology via NDIS or private; Medicare CDM item for private
- Early language intervention: Hanen programme "It Takes Two to Talk" for parents of late talkers; evidence-based

**Developmental paediatrician:**
- Refer for: ASD diagnostic assessment; GDD; ADHD diagnosis in young children; complex neurodevelopmental presentation
- Wait times: often 6–18 months in public system; private developmental paediatrician faster
- NDIS ECEI can provide supports before formal diagnosis (while waiting)

**Autism-specific management:**
- Applied Behaviour Analysis (ABA) and Naturalistic Developmental Behavioural Interventions (NDBI) — evidence-based early interventions
- NDIS funding for ABA/NDBI therapy under ECEI or participant plan
- Speech pathology: AAC (augmentative and alternative communication) for non-verbal children
- OT: sensory processing; daily living skills
- Early Start Denver Model (ESDM): evidence-based early intervention for toddlers with ASD; RCH and specialist autism centres

**Parent and family support:**
- Raising Children Network (raisingchildren.net.au): Australian government-funded; evidence-based parenting resources
- Autism Spectrum Australia (Aspect): autismspectrum.org.au; parent training; school support
- Down Syndrome Australia: downsyndrome.org.au
- Positive Behaviour Support: NDIS-funded for challenging behaviours

## Paediatric Context and Guardian Involvement

- Family-centred approach: parents are the primary drivers of early intervention; their concerns must be taken seriously; parental concern is the most sensitive predictor of developmental problems
- Cultural context: developmental milestones may vary; bilingual or multilingual families — speech development in bilingual children follows different but normal trajectories; do NOT mistake bilingualism for delay
- School readiness: at 4.5–5 years before Kindergarten entry — assess school readiness; recommend developmental paediatric assessment if concerns
- Transition planning: NDIS transition from ECEI (0–6) to individual NDIS plan at age 6; GP assists with supporting documentation
- Vaccination schedule: developmental concerns do NOT affect standard immunisation schedule; vaccinate on time

## Prescribing Considerations (AU PBS)

- **No medication is standard first-line for developmental delay or ASD** (behavioural interventions and therapy are primary)
- **Methylphenidate (Ritalin, Concerta) / Dexamphetamine**: ADHD (after formal diagnosis by paediatrician or psychiatrist); PBS-restricted Schedule 8; paediatric prescriber initiates; GP may continue scripts after specialist review
- **Melatonin** (sleep difficulties in ASD/ADHD; discussed under `insomnia`); widely used off-label; compounded or Circadin (≥55 years — off-label in children); often helpful; low-dose starting at 0.5 mg
- **Risperidone** (PBS restricted: severe ASD-associated irritability and aggression; specialist initiation; monitor for metabolic syndrome, EPS, QTc prolongation; second-generation antipsychotic)
- **Note:** NDIS funding is the most impactful "prescription" for children with developmental concerns — facilitating NDIS access through GP referral letters, diagnostic reports, and supporting documentation is a critical GP role.

## Escalation Triggers

**Urgent:**
- Regression of any developmental milestone: urgent developmental paediatrician assessment; MRI brain if neurological concern
- Febrile seizure with developmental concerns: emergency paediatric assessment
- Suspected child abuse or neglect contributing to developmental delay: mandatory report; Child Protection Services

**Non-urgent referral:**
- Any developmental concern: NDIS ECEI referral (0–6 years); developmental paediatrician; allied health via CDM
- Positive M-CHAT-R or ASD features: developmental paediatrician; referral within 1–2 months
- Speech delay of any degree: speech pathology referral promptly

## Telehealth Suitability

**Suitable for telehealth:**
- Discussion of developmental concerns with parents; advice on stimulation activities
- NDIS referral paperwork; letter writing
- Result review from assessments
- Medication management (ADHD, melatonin) for established patients

**Requires in-person assessment:**
- Developmental observation: child engagement, social behaviour, play, communication cannot be adequately assessed via video
- Formal developmental screening (Ages and Stages, M-CHAT-R): ideally in-person with observation
- Hearing and vision screening
