## Fitness for Work Assessment (RACGP)

Fitness for work (FFW) assessments and medical certificates are common GP tasks, encompassing a spectrum from routine sickness absence certificates to complex return-to-work (RTW) coordination, occupational medical assessments, and fitness for specific duties. GPs must balance patient advocacy with professional obligations: certifying only what is clinically supported, protecting worker rights, and avoiding complicity in fraudulent claims. The AMA Position Statement on Medical Certificates outlines GP obligations. Return to work (RTW) after illness or injury should be facilitated as early as safely possible — early graded return reduces the risk of chronicity, deconditioning, and loss of work identity. Workers' compensation (WorkCover) operates under state/territory legislation (each state has its own system: WorkSafe Victoria, WorkCover QLD, etc.); Comcare covers Commonwealth employees; the National Disability Insurance Scheme (NDIS) and Centrelink Disability Support Pension (DSP) require separate assessment processes. Telehealth is suitable for most FFW consultations after initial face-to-face assessment.

## Clinical Presentation

**Routine sickness absence (medical certificate):**
- Employee requires GP medical certificate for sickness absence under the Fair Work Act 2009
- Entitlement: 10 days paid personal/carer's leave per year for full-time employees
- GP obligation: certify based on clinical assessment; state: "unfit for work from [date] to [date]"; specify if modified duties appropriate; cannot certify absence without examining the patient (or telehealth equivalent)
- GP does NOT need to specify diagnosis on certificate (employer not entitled to diagnosis); state "a medical condition" if patient requests confidentiality

**WorkCover (workers' compensation) medical certificates:**
- Worker injured at work or develops occupational disease
- GP is "nominated treating doctor" (NTD): coordinate treatment; complete WorkCover certificates
- Initial certificate of capacity: describes nature of injury; capacity for work (fit/unfit/modified duties); treatment plan; WorkCover claim number
- Progress certificate: every 2–4 weeks; assess recovery; modify capacity; outline RTW plan
- States: WorkSafe Victoria (1800 136 089); icare NSW; WorkCover QLD; ReturnToWork SA; WorkCover WA; Comcare (federal)
- GP role: not to adjudicate liability (that is the insurer's role); provide honest, clinically based assessments

**Fitness for specific duties (occupational assessment):**
- Commercial vehicle drivers: annual medical for licence class (Bus, Truck, Taxi); AUSTROADS Medical Standards for Licensing; epilepsy, cardiac disease, diabetes, vision, sleep apnoea all affect licensing
- Commercial pilots (CASA): CASA-designated aviation medical examiners (DAME); not routine GP
- Seafarers: Maritime Safety Authority; specific medical standards
- Police, fire, ambulance, defence: service-specific medical examination processes
- High-risk industries: mining, construction, offshore — pre-employment and periodic medicals

**Return to work planning (best practice):**
- Early return to modified duties: superior outcomes compared to waiting for "full recovery"
- Graded return: hours and duties gradually increased over weeks
- Workplace modifications: ergonomic adjustments; task modification; graduated physical demands
- Biopsychosocial model: pain, function, beliefs, employer relationship, fear of re-injury all affect RTW outcomes
- "Fit Note" approach (UK model, increasingly used in Australia): focus on WHAT the patient CAN do, not just what they cannot

## Assessment Approach

**Standard sickness certificate consultation:**
- Clinical assessment: diagnosis or condition; severity; functional limitations
- Duration estimate: based on clinical evidence; avoid open-ended certificates ("indefinitely")
- Modified duties consideration: always consider if partial work capacity exists; most beneficial outcome for worker
- No examination requirement for telephone/telehealth certificate for follow-up of established condition (COVID era rule changes expanded telehealth scope)

**WorkCover capacity assessment:**
- Establish: nature of injury/disease; diagnosis; causal link to work (WorkCover's role to determine; GP provides clinical facts)
- Capacity for work: full duty / modified duty (specify restrictions: no heavy lifting >5 kg, no prolonged standing >30 min) / no capacity for work
- Treatment plan: physiotherapy, medication, specialist referral; expected timeline
- WorkCover WIQ (Work Injury Questionnaire) and state-specific forms: complete accurately
- Avoid: overstating incapacity (hinders recovery); understating incapacity (re-injury risk)
- Conflict of interest: if patient's employer is a patient → consider declaring conflict; cannot certify employee-employer dispute impartially

**AUSTROADS fitness to drive (key conditions):**
- Sleep apnoea: untreated moderate-severe OSA = UNFIT for commercial vehicle licence; private licence = UNFIT until AHI <10 on CPAP; AUSTROADS Medical Standards Table B
- Epilepsy: seizure-free period required (private: 12 months for most; commercial: 5 years or 10 years depending on seizure type); neurologist to certify
- Cardiac conditions: acute MI, cardiac surgery, significant arrhythmia — conditional driving restrictions; AUSTROADS Table A
- Diabetes with hypoglycaemia risk: commercial drivers with insulin → significant restrictions; private licence → AUSTROADS guidelines; document A1c and hypoglycaemia history
- Vision: visual acuity standards; visual field standards; monocular vision restrictions

**Centrelink assessments (DSP, JobSeeker):**
- Treating doctor's report (TDR): clinical summary for disability assessment; not a determination of eligibility
- Job Capacity Assessment (JCA): conducted by government assessors using TDR information
- DSP (Disability Support Pension): permanent impairment ≥20 points; impairment tables; specialist assessments often required
- JobSeeker mutual obligation exemptions: temporary incapacity; GP certifies inability to participate in activities

## Key Considerations

**Medical certificate obligations:**
- Certify only what is clinically justifiable: backdating certificates is acceptable if the patient was seen and assessed; fabricating or exaggerating incapacity = professional misconduct
- Certification period: based on expected recovery; typical guidelines: cold/URTI 1–3 days; influenza 5–7 days; low back strain 1–2 weeks; moderate depression 2–6 weeks
- Private vs WorkCover: different certificate types; WorkCover certificates more detailed
- Confidentiality: employer NOT entitled to diagnosis; "medical condition" acceptable
- AMA position: GPs should not certify patients they have not assessed; backdating without clinical basis = fraudulent

**Independent medical examinations (IME):**
- Arranged by insurer or employer to provide independent opinion
- Examining doctor has no treating relationship with patient; advise patient accordingly
- GP should inform patient of their rights (right to have a support person; right to obtain a copy of the report)

**Occupational health and safety (OHS) reporting obligations:**
- Notifiable incidents: serious workplace injuries, dangerous incidents — Safe Work Australia; employer must notify state OHS regulator; GP role: documentation; treating injured worker
- COVID-19 and infectious disease in workplace: specific notification requirements; occupational exposure history

## Management Principles

**RTW coordination best practice:**
- Establish treating team: GP (nominated treating doctor), physiotherapist, occupational therapist, psychologist, workplace
- Set realistic RTW goals and timeline: discussed with patient and ideally with employer or RTW coordinator
- Identify barriers to RTW: fear of re-injury, pain catastrophising, workplace relationship issues, employer attitudes, inadequate workplace modification
- Psychological screening: PHQ-9 for depression; pain catastrophising scale (PCS); early intervention for psychosocial "yellow flags" (work dissatisfaction, catastrophising) predicts delayed RTW
- Graded RTW plan: document in WorkCover certificate (hours: 4hr/day initially; duties: light duties, no heavy lifting; gradual escalation)

**GP communication with stakeholders:**
- Employer: general functional restrictions (no diagnosis without consent); capacity for duty and modifications needed
- WorkCover insurer: certificate of capacity (standard form); progress certificates; treatment requests
- Patient: primary advocate; explain WorkCover process; medical certificate content and limits; RTW plan
- Specialists: referral with occupational context; outcome reporting back to GP

**Psychological aspects of work incapacity:**
- Work has health benefits: income, structure, social connection, purpose; early RTW improves outcomes
- Psychological "yellow flags" (high-risk for delayed RTW): pain catastrophising, depression, fear-avoidance beliefs, high pain behaviour, perceived injustice
- Brief psychological intervention: CBT referral; pain management programme; GP Mental Health Treatment Plan
- Consider: workplace relationship issues; bullying; job dissatisfaction — may require HR and occupational health involvement beyond medical management

## Prescribing Considerations (AU PBS)

- **Medical Certificates**: not a prescribing matter; certifying fitness for work is an exercise of clinical judgement documented in a certificate
- **Pain management for WorkCover injuries**: follow standard `chronic-pain` or specific condition guidelines; document in WorkCover treatment plan; obtain insurer approval for specialist referrals, investigations, and ongoing allied health
- **Psychological treatment**: GP Mental Health Treatment Plan for workers with comorbid psychological disturbance affecting RTW; Better Access (up to 20 sessions/year); WorkCover may also fund psychological treatment (apply for approval)
- **Note:** For WorkCover claims, treatments beyond GP consultations (physiotherapy, psychology, specialist review) typically require WorkCover insurer approval; submit request via WorkCover treatment request form. Unapproved treatment costs fall to patient.

## Escalation Triggers

**Urgent:**
- Workplace injury requiring emergency care: 000 + workplace accident documentation
- Mental health crisis in workers' compensation patient: suicidality + inability to work + comorbid depression = acute Mental Health Plan; crisis team referral if at risk

**Non-urgent referral:**
- Occupational physician: complex FFW assessments; commercial driver medicals; AUSTROADS fitness to drive for complex conditions; independent medical examinations
- Physiotherapy/OT: RTW functional assessment; workplace ergonomic visit; graded RTW program
- Pain clinic: complex workers' compensation pain; biopsychosocial assessment; interdisciplinary management
- Employment support: Disability Employment Services (DES) for patients not RTW (Centrelink DES providers)

## Telehealth Suitability

**Suitable for telehealth:**
- Follow-up WorkCover progress certificates (established patient; known injury; reviewing recovery)
- Centrelink medical assessment documentation
- Medical certificate consultation (telehealth equivalent of consultation)
- RTW planning discussions; psychosocial support
- AUSTROADS consultation for known conditions already assessed in-person

**Requires in-person assessment:**
- Initial WorkCover injury assessment: physical examination
- AUSTROADS fitness to drive assessment: vision testing, clinical examination
- Pre-employment medicals: physical fitness standards
- Any new injury or major change in clinical status
