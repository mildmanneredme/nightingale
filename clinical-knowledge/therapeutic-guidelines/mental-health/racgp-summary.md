## Mental Health Assessment (RACGP)

Mental health conditions including depression, anxiety, and stress-related disorders are highly prevalent in Australian primary care. General practice plays a central role in identification, management, and coordination of care for mental health conditions. Telehealth can facilitate access to mental health support, particularly in rural and remote areas.

## Common presentations

**Depression**: Low mood, loss of interest or pleasure (anhedonia), fatigue, sleep disturbance, appetite changes, poor concentration, feelings of worthlessness or guilt. Validated screening tools include the PHQ-9 and K10. Assessment of suicidal ideation is essential.

**Anxiety disorders**: Excessive worry, restlessness, fatigue, difficulty concentrating, irritability, muscle tension, sleep disturbance. Generalised anxiety disorder (GAD), panic disorder, and social anxiety are common in primary care.

**Adjustment disorders**: Emotional or behavioural symptoms in response to identifiable stressors, typically resolving within 6 months once the stressor resolves.

## Safety and risk assessment

**Suicidal ideation must always be assessed**. If a patient expresses thoughts of self-harm or suicide:
- Acknowledge and take seriously — do not dismiss
- Ask directly about intent, plan, and means
- Assess protective factors
- Immediate safety planning is required
- Patients at acute risk require immediate escalation (000 or emergency department)

**Crisis resources (Australia)**:
- Lifeline: 13 11 14 (24/7)
- Beyond Blue: 1300 22 4636
- Suicide Call Back Service: 1300 659 467

## Management principles

- Collaborative care planning between GP, patient, and mental health clinicians
- Psychological therapies (CBT, mindfulness) are first-line for mild-moderate depression and anxiety
- Mental Health Treatment Plans (Medicare item 2700 series) enable GP-referred allied mental health sessions
- Pharmacotherapy for moderate-severe depression: prescribing requires GP clinical assessment and sign-off
- Regular monitoring and review of treatment response
- Lifestyle interventions: regular physical activity, sleep hygiene, social connection, reduction of alcohol and substance use

## Telehealth considerations

Mental health presentations can be well-suited to telehealth, but clinicians should be aware of:
- Limitations in assessing non-verbal cues and affect via video or voice
- Risk of inadequate privacy and safety at the patient's location
- The need for a clear safety plan including local emergency contacts before ending the consultation
