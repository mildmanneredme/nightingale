## UTI Assessment (RACGP)

Urinary tract infections (UTIs) are among the most common bacterial infections in primary care. Uncomplicated lower UTI (cystitis) in non-pregnant adult women can often be managed with short-course antibiotics following clinical assessment. UTI diagnosis in men, pregnant women, children, and catheterised patients is more complex and requires GP clinical assessment.

## Common presentations

Symptoms of uncomplicated lower UTI include dysuria (painful urination), urinary frequency and urgency, suprapubic discomfort, and cloudy or malodorous urine. Haematuria (blood in urine) may also be present. Fever, loin pain, nausea, and rigors suggest upper UTI (pyelonephritis) and require urgent assessment.

## Investigations

Urine dipstick testing provides a rapid assessment. Urine microscopy and culture (MCS) is recommended to confirm diagnosis and guide antibiotic choice, particularly given increasing antibiotic resistance patterns. Mid-stream urine (MSU) collection technique should be explained to patients.

## Management considerations

First-line antibiotic options for uncomplicated cystitis are determined by local resistance patterns and should follow current Australian antibiotic guidelines (Therapeutic Guidelines: Antibiotic). Prescribing is the responsibility of the reviewing GP. Treatment duration varies by presentation (typically 3 days for uncomplicated cystitis in women).

Asymptomatic bacteriuria generally does not require antibiotic treatment except in pregnancy. Over-treatment contributes to antimicrobial resistance.

## Escalation triggers

Patients should seek urgent medical attention if they experience:
- Fever above 38.5°C, rigors, or feeling systemically unwell
- Loin or flank pain (possible pyelonephritis)
- Nausea or vomiting preventing oral hydration or medication
- Symptoms not improving within 48 hours of antibiotic commencement
- Recurrent UTIs (3 or more per year)

Call 000 or go to the nearest emergency department immediately for: high fever with confusion, severe loin pain with rigors, or signs of sepsis.
