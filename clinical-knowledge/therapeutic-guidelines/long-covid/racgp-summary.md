## Long COVID / Post-COVID-19 Condition Assessment (RACGP)

Post-COVID-19 condition (Long COVID) is defined by the WHO as the continuation or development of new symptoms 3 months after the initial COVID-19 infection, lasting at least 2 months and not explained by an alternative diagnosis. It affects an estimated 10–20% of people infected with SARS-CoV-2, with higher rates in women, unvaccinated individuals, and those with more severe initial illness. Long COVID represents a significant emerging burden on Australian general practice. The GP is the primary coordinator of Long COVID assessment, investigation, and management. There is no single test for Long COVID — it is a clinical diagnosis of exclusion. Telehealth is well-suited for Long COVID assessment and ongoing management.

## Clinical Presentation

Long COVID has over 200 reported symptoms. The most common and clinically significant are:

**Fatigue and post-exertional malaise (PEM):**
- Most prevalent symptom; often debilitating; not improved by rest
- PEM: worsening of symptoms following physical, cognitive, or emotional exertion; may be delayed 12–48 hours after activity; hallmark feature (also seen in ME/CFS — see `chronic-fatigue` entry)
- Graded activity increase must be done very cautiously due to PEM risk

**Cognitive symptoms ("brain fog"):**
- Difficulty concentrating, memory impairment, word-finding difficulty, slowed cognition
- Often impacts work, study, and daily functioning
- Validated with Montreal Cognitive Assessment (MoCA) or other cognitive screening

**Respiratory:**
- Breathlessness (exertional dyspnoea) persisting despite resolved acute infection
- Chest tightness; chronic cough
- Objective hypoxia unusual in Long COVID without concurrent lung pathology

**Cardiac:**
- Palpitations, racing heart, chest pain
- Postural tachycardia syndrome (POTS): tachycardia >30 bpm on standing, presyncope, dizziness, fatigue; increasingly recognised as Long COVID complication
- Myocarditis (rare): elevated troponin, ECG changes

**Other common symptoms:**
- Sleep disturbance: insomnia or hypersomnia
- Anosmia/parosmia: persistent smell dysfunction or distortion (parosmia — altered smell perception)
- Autonomic dysfunction: dizziness, POTS, temperature dysregulation
- Musculoskeletal: joint and muscle pain
- Mood: anxiety, depression (may be primary or secondary to disability from Long COVID)
- Headache; gastrointestinal symptoms; menstrual cycle changes

## Assessment Approach

**Diagnosis — exclusion of alternatives is essential:**
History: onset timing relative to COVID-19 infection; characterise each symptom; severity and functional impact; timeline of initial illness and vaccination status; prior health; medication history.

**Structured symptom assessment:**
- Fatigue: Fatigue Severity Scale (FSS); Chalder Fatigue Scale
- Breathlessness: Modified Medical Research Council (mMRC) dyspnoea scale
- Cognitive function: MoCA; online validated tools
- Autonomic symptoms: POTS screening — standing heart rate test (active stand test: 10 minutes lying, then HR on standing; rise of ≥30 bpm suggests POTS)
- Mental health: PHQ-9, GAD-7
- Functional status and work impact

**Investigations (to exclude alternative diagnoses and guide organ-specific management):**
- FBC: anaemia, lymphopenia
- UEC, LFTs, thyroid (TSH): systemic assessment
- Ferritin, CRP: inflammatory markers; iron deficiency
- Troponin, ECG: if cardiac symptoms; echocardiogram if troponin raised
- Chest X-ray or CT: if persistent respiratory symptoms — rule out organising pneumonia, fibrosis
- Spirometry: assess lung function — FVC, FEV1
- Holter monitor: if palpitations / suspected POTS arrhythmia
- D-dimer: only if PE suspected (not routinely for Long COVID fatigue)
- Blood glucose, HbA1c: new-onset diabetes (post-COVID association)
- ANA, ANCA: if autoimmune features

**Functional assessment:** Occupational therapist assessment for work capacity and daily activity limitation; relevant for return-to-work and disability documentation.

## Key Differentials

- ME/CFS (Myalgic Encephalomyelitis / Chronic Fatigue Syndrome): overlapping syndrome; same management principles (pacing, PEM avoidance); Long COVID and ME/CFS share significant phenotypic overlap; see `chronic-fatigue` entry
- Depression or anxiety: common comorbidity; distinguish from PEM and fatigue of Long COVID; treat concurrently; PHQ-9, GAD-7
- New thyroid disease: hypothyroidism causes fatigue, cognitive symptoms — check TSH
- Iron deficiency anaemia: fatigue, dyspnoea — common; check ferritin; treat
- Cardiac complications: persistent myocarditis, arrhythmia — POTS; investigate if cardiac symptoms prominent
- Underlying lung pathology: organising pneumonia, fibrosis — CT, spirometry
- New-onset POTS unrelated to COVID-19: distinguish — timeline critical

## Management Principles

**Central principles:**
- Validate and believe the patient: Long COVID is a genuine condition; avoid dismissive responses
- Individualised, patient-centred approach: no single protocol; adapt to patient's symptom profile
- Multidisciplinary care: GP coordinates respiratory, cardiology, psychology, rehabilitation as needed
- Set realistic expectations: recovery is typically slow and non-linear; many improve substantially over months to years

**Pacing and activity management (key — especially given PEM):**
- Pacing: activity within the "energy envelope" — not exceeding capacity that triggers PEM; occupational therapist guidance
- Graded exercise therapy (GET): NOT recommended in Long COVID (as in ME/CFS) — can worsen PEM; avoid pushing through fatigue
- Heart rate monitoring (wearable): helps patients identify exertion thresholds that trigger PEM
- Cognitive pacing: brain fog also subject to PEM — limit cognitive demands during recovery

**Symptom-specific management:**

Fatigue: pacing (as above); sleep hygiene; iron supplementation if deficient; treat coexisting depression/anaemia.

POTS management:
- Increase fluid intake (2–3 L/day) and salt intake (5–10 g/day — unless contraindicated)
- Compression stockings (30–40 mmHg)
- Head-of-bed elevation (10–15 degrees)
- Graded physical reconditioning in recumbent/seated position (supine cycling, recumbent rowing) — not upright exercise initially
- Pharmacotherapy (specialist supervised): low-dose beta-blocker (propranolol 10–20 mg twice daily) reduces heart rate on standing; fludrocortisone (volume expansion); ivabradine (sinus node If channel blocker — off-label POTS)

Anosmia/parosmia: olfactory training (smell retraining with different odour groups daily for months); spontaneous improvement common but slow.

Cognitive symptoms (brain fog): cognitive pacing; address sleep, depression, iron deficiency; occupational therapy; neuropsychology assessment for severe impairment.

Respiratory symptoms: assess for underlying pathology first; breathing physiotherapy if no structural cause.

Mood disorders: CBT, SSRI/SNRI for comorbid anxiety/depression; see `anxiety` and `depression` entries; Long COVID itself may respond to treatment of depression.

## Prescribing Considerations (AU PBS)

- **Propranolol** (PBS unrestricted — POTS management in Long COVID; 10–20 mg twice daily; trial of beta-blocker for tachycardia and POTS symptoms)
- **Fludrocortisone** (PBS restricted: orthostatic hypotension; 0.05–0.1 mg daily; specialist supervised for POTS)
- **Iron supplements** (PBS restricted: iron deficiency; treat if ferritin low or deficient)
- **SSRI/SNRI** (PBS restricted: comorbid anxiety/depression in Long COVID — see `anxiety`, `depression` entries)
- **Note:** There is currently no proven specific pharmacotherapy for Long COVID itself; treatment targets specific organ manifestations and comorbid conditions. Clinical trials ongoing.

## Escalation Triggers

**Urgent assessment:**
- Chest pain + elevated troponin — myocarditis; cardiologist urgently
- Severe POTS with syncope — haemodynamic assessment; cardiology
- Progressive breathlessness with hypoxia — pulmonology; CT chest

**Non-urgent specialist referral:**
- Cardiologist: POTS, myocarditis, palpitations with ECG/Holter abnormality
- Respiratory physician: persistent breathlessness, abnormal spirometry or CT
- Neurologist: severe cognitive dysfunction, autonomic neuropathy
- Psychologist: CBT for fatigue management, anxiety, depression
- Occupational therapist: functional assessment, pacing program, return-to-work

## Telehealth Suitability

Long COVID management is well suited to telehealth.

**Suitable for telehealth:**
- Symptom inventory and validated scale administration (FSS, mMRC, MoCA can be patient-completed prior)
- Blood test result review (ferritin, thyroid, cardiac markers)
- Pacing education and activity management counselling
- Mental health assessment and management plan
- Pharmacotherapy prescribing (propranolol, SSRI, iron supplements)
- Referral coordination (cardiology, pulmonology, occupational therapy)
- Ongoing monitoring and support

**Requires in-person assessment:**
- Active stand test for POTS (requires BP and HR measurement in lying and standing positions)
- Spirometry, ECG, echo — arrange via pathology or specialist
- Physical examination for specific organ involvement
