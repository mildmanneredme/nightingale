## RACGP Red Book — Preventive Screening Summary

The RACGP Red Book (Guidelines for Preventive Activities in General Practice, 10th Edition, 2023) provides evidence-based recommendations for preventive activities across the lifespan. This summary provides a structured overview for Nightingale clinical AI retrieval purposes.

## Cardiovascular Risk

**Absolute cardiovascular risk (CVD risk) assessment:**
- Who: All adults 45–79 years without existing CVD; Indigenous Australians from 30 years
- Frequency: Every 2 years
- Tools: Australian CVD Risk Calculator (cvdcheck.org.au); Australian Absolute CVD Risk Charts
- Components: age, sex, BP, total cholesterol, HDL-C, smoking status, diabetes, family history
- Risk thresholds: Low (<10%), moderate (10–14%), high (≥15%) 10-year CVD event risk
- Action: High risk (≥15%) → lifestyle + lipid-lowering ± antihypertensive; moderate risk → individual discussion; see `cardiovascular-risk` and `hypertension`

**Blood pressure screening:**
- Adults ≥18: measure opportunistically every 2 years if normal; annually if borderline/elevated
- Target: <130/80 mmHg for most adults (2022 Heart Foundation guidelines)

**Cholesterol screening:**
- Adults ≥45: fasting lipid profile as part of CVD risk assessment
- Start earlier if: family history dyslipidaemia, familial hypercholesterolaemia, diabetes, existing CVD risk factors

## Diabetes Screening

**Type 2 diabetes screening:**
- Risk assessment: AUSDRISK tool — Australian Type 2 Diabetes Risk Assessment (health.gov.au/ausdrisk)
- AUSDRISK score ≥12: fasting BSL or HbA1c; rescreen in 3 years if normal
- High-risk groups: screen earlier — Indigenous Australians (≥35), overweight/obese patients, gestational diabetes history, polycystic ovary syndrome, first-degree relative with T2D
- Prediabetes: fasting glucose 5.6–6.9 mmol/L or HbA1c 39–47 mmol/mol → lifestyle intervention; metformin in some; annual monitoring

## Cancer Screening Programs

**Cervical cancer (National Cervical Screening Programme — NCSP):**
- Who: All people with a cervix 25–74 years (regardless of sexual history or vaccination status)
- Test: Cervical Screening Test (CST) — HPV primary testing with liquid-based cytology reflex
- Frequency: 5-yearly (after 2 consecutive annual Pap smears were replaced in 2017)
- Exit test at 70–74 years; after 3 consecutive negative tests
- Re-entry after hysterectomy: only if cervix retained
- Results registry: National Cancer Screening Register; automated recall letters
- HPV vaccination: Gardasil 9 reduces cervical cancer risk; does NOT affect screening schedule

**Breast cancer (BreastScreen Australia):**
- Who: Women 50–74 years (invited); women 40–49 and 75+ (self-refer; not routinely invited)
- Test: 2-view mammography, free
- Frequency: 2-yearly
- Contact: BreastScreen Australia 13 20 50; state BreastScreen services
- High-risk surveillance: Women with BRCA1/2 pathogenic variant; Familial Cancer Centre referral; annual MRI from age 30

**Bowel cancer (National Bowel Cancer Screening Programme — NBCSP):**
- Who: All Australians 45–74 years (from 2024 — extended from 50–74)
- Test: immunochemical faecal occult blood test (iFOBT) sent by post every 2 years
- Action on positive iFOBT: colonoscopy urgently (regardless of other symptoms)
- Participation rate: approximately 40%; encourage all eligible patients
- High-risk surveillance: personal/family history CRC or polyps → colonoscopy at shorter intervals (see `bowel-cancer-screening`)

**Prostate cancer:**
- No national screening programme; PSA testing controversial
- RACGP Red Book: shared decision-making; PSA for men aged 50–69 who are interested after informed discussion of benefits and harms; earlier if family history or African ancestry
- See `prostate-cancer-screening`

**Lung cancer (National Lung Cancer Screening Programme — NLCSP):**
- Commenced mid-2025 in Australia: annual low-dose CT (LDCT) for eligible high-risk adults
- Eligibility: age 50–70; ≥30 pack-years smoking history; current or ex-smoker (quit <15 years)
- Implementation: GP referral; see cancer screening updates

**Skin cancer:**
- No national population screening programme for skin cancer in Australia
- Opportunistic examination: annual total body skin check recommended for high-risk patients (fair skin, family/personal history of melanoma, high UV exposure, immunosuppressed)
- Medicare skin check: no dedicated skin check item; opportunistic during general consultation; 3-monthly surveillance post melanoma
- Dermoscopy: supports detection; GP training available; RACGP online dermoscopy courses

## Immunisation (National Immunisation Program — NIP)

**Childhood NIP schedule key points:**
- Birth: Hepatitis B
- 6 weeks, 4 months, 6 months: DTaP-IPV-Hib-HepB (Infanrix Hexa); rotavirus (Rotarix); PCV13 (Prevenar)
- 12 months: MMR (Priorix); MenACWY (Nimenrix); PCV13 booster
- 18 months: DTaP (Infanrix); MMR-V (Priorix Tetra); MenB (Bexsero)
- 4 years: DTaP-IPV (Infanrix-IPV)
- 12–13 years: HPV (Gardasil 9) — 2-dose schedule
- 17–18 years: dTpa booster (Boostrix)
- Australian Immunisation Register (AIR): all vaccinations documented; GP access via Medicare and clinical software

**Adult immunisation:**
- Influenza: annual; NIP-funded for: ≥65 years, pregnant women, Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander ≥6 months, children 6 months to <5 years with medical risk conditions; people with chronic medical conditions
- Pneumococcal: 13-valent (PCV13) and 23-valent (23vPPV); NIP-funded ≥70 years (previously ≥65); immunocompromised patients
- Zoster (Shingrix — recombinant zoster vaccine): NIP-funded for adults ≥65 years and Aboriginal/Torres Strait Islander ≥50 years; 2-dose schedule (0 and 2–6 months)
- COVID-19: boosters as per ATAGI recommendations; check current ATAGI guidance
- RSV: RSVpreF vaccine (Abrysvo) — funded for adults ≥75 years (NIP 2024); single dose; see current NIP schedule

## Developmental Screening and Child Health

**Milestones surveillance:**
- MCHN (Maternal and Child Health Nurse) contacts: standard times (see `developmental-concerns`)
- Recommended ages for developmental surveillance in GP: 6 weeks, 4 months, 12 months, 18 months, 2 years, 3 years, 4 years (prior to school entry)
- ASQ-3 (Ages and Stages Questionnaire): parent-completed; standardised developmental screening

**Hearing:**
- Newborn Hearing Screening (all Australian states): AABR or OAE screening at birth
- 4-year school entry hearing screen (state health programmes)

**Vision:**
- 3.5-year vision screen (child health check): amblyopia detection; Hirschberg test; cover test
- School entry vision (state education programmes)

## Mental Health Screening

**Depression screening:**
- Adults: PHQ-9 opportunistically; Edinburgh Postnatal Depression Scale (EPDS) at 6 weeks and 6 months postnatally
- Adolescents: K10, PHQ-A
- No national routine depression screening programme; RACGP recommends opportunistic screening in at-risk groups

**Alcohol screening:**
- AUDIT-C: short 3-item screen; recommend in general practice opportunistically
- NHMRC 2020 guidelines: no more than 10 standard drinks per week; no more than 4 on any single day

## Chronic Disease Management

**Osteoporosis / fracture risk:**
- Women ≥65 and men ≥70: bone density (DEXA) assessment and FRAX fracture risk score
- Younger if: prolonged corticosteroid use, premature menopause, fragility fracture history

**Thyroid:**
- No routine thyroid screening in asymptomatic adults; TSH if symptoms or risk factors

**Kidney disease:**
- CKD risk assessment: urinalysis (ACR) and eGFR in high-risk groups (diabetes, hypertension, family history CKD, Indigenous Australians)

## Reproductive Health

**Antenatal screening:**
- All pregnancies: blood group, FBC, rubella immunity, hepatitis B, HIV, syphilis (RPR), urine MCS
- Universal GBS (Group B Streptococcus) swab at 35–37 weeks
- First trimester: combined screening (PAPP-A + nuchal translucency + hCG) for chromosomal anomaly
- Gestational diabetes: 75 g OGTT at 24–28 weeks (or first trimester if high risk)

**Preconception:**
- Folic acid 400–500 µg daily: at least 1 month pre-conception and first trimester; 5 mg if neural tube defect history
- Varicella immunity check; rubella immunity
